Aurélien Ghiglino

Applying AI and machine learning to aerospace system design, with a focus on aircraft design, modeling, and data-driven engineering.

About

I work on integrating machine learning into the design and analysis of complex aerospace systems. My focus is on combining physics-based models with data-driven methods to improve performance, robustness, and design efficiency.

I am currently at Stanford’s Aerospace Design Lab (ADL), where I work primarily on aircraft design, and explore applications of these methods to space systems and planetary environments.

Focus Areas

  • Aircraft design and aerospace systems engineering
  • Machine learning for engineering design
  • Surrogate modeling and generative methods
  • Multimodal data integration and simulation
  • Applications to space systems and lunar environments

Current Work

My primary work focuses on aircraft design methodologies, including the use of machine learning to accelerate design space exploration and improve system-level optimization.

In parallel, I explore how similar approaches can be applied to lunar subsurface mapping and resource detection, particularly in data-sparse and high-uncertainty environments.
